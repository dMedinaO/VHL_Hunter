  • Mutation

    Indicates the change in the DNA/Protein sequence. Depending on the type of mutation, the nomenclature changes. For protein-type mutations, the suffix "p." Is prepended. For DNA (Coding Sequence) mutations, the prefix "c." comes before.

  • Molecule

    Indicates the mutated macromolecule. It can refer to DNA or Proteins

  • Mutation Type

    Indicates the exchange rate in the specified sequence.

      Protein Mutations

    • Missense

      A protein that simply replace an aminoacid by another, in an specific position.

      The nomenclature starts with the "p." sufix. Follows the wild type aminoacid, the position of this one and finish with the mutated aminoacid.

      Example:

      p.A132T

    • Nonsense

      A nonsense mutation is a truncated protein.

      The nomenclature starts with the "p." sufix. Follows the final aminoacid and his position. It finish with an "X" that indicates a stop codon.

      Example:

      p.A132X

    • Silent

      A silent mutation corresponds to a mutation that changes the gene, but does not change the amino acid sequence.

      It follows the same nomenclature than a missense mutation, except that the wild and mutated aminoacids are the same.

      Example:

      p.A132A
